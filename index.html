Research Lines

Our work combines experimental data with computational theory to bridge the gap between brain and behavior.

1. Social Prediction & Theory of Mind

We examine how people understand and predict others’ actions and intentions. Our research seeks to map the neural architectures that support mentalizing and the simulation of other minds.

2. Value Computation & Decision Control

Investigating how self–other distinctions shape value computation. We explore how the brain exerts control over decisions when the outcomes affect others versus oneself.

3. Motivation & Learning Strategies

Studying how motivational systems modulate social learning. We use computational reinforcement learning models to quantify how motivation alters learning rates and exploration strategies.

4. Lifespan & Clinical Populations

Analyzing how these processes change across the lifespan and in subclinical populations. We aim to identify biomarkers of social cognitive decline or dysfunction.
