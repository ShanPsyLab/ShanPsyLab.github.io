SHAN Lab

Our Focus

"Our lab unravels the brain mechanisms driving social decision-making, with a distinct focus on how these cognitive processes adapt with age and impact human health and wellbeing."

Research Areas

We investigate the intersection of cognition, social behavior, and neuroscience.

Social Decision-Making

We explore how individuals make choices in social contexts, how they learn from others, and the underlying neurocomputational mechanisms that guide pro-social behaviors and trust.

Cognitive Aging & Health

Investigating how learning and decision-making capabilities evolve across the lifespan. We aim to identify neural markers that predict healthy cognitive aging versus decline.

Neuroimaging (fMRI/EEG)

Utilizing advanced functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) and EEG techniques to map the brain networks responsible for complex cognitive and social tasks.

Computational Modeling

Applying reinforcement learning and Bayesian models to formalize theories of human cognition, providing a quantitative bridge between observed behavior and brain activity.

Learning Resources

A curated collection of the most highly recommended tutorials, courses, and tools for learning fMRI data analysis and neuroimaging.

Andy's Brain Book

Created by Dr. Andrew Jahn, this is arguably the best step-by-step practical guide for beginners. It covers complete pipelines for fMRI processing using major software like SPM, FSL, AFNI, and FreeSurfer.

Visit Tutorial

Principles of fMRI (Coursera)

Taught by experts Tor Wager and Martin Lindquist. This course provides a fantastic theoretical foundation covering MR physics, BOLD hemodynamics, experimental design, and general linear models (GLM).

View Course

NIH MRI Summer Course

A comprehensive collection of lecture videos and slides from the National Institutes of Health. It dives deeply into advanced structural and functional imaging concepts.

Watch Lectures

Neurohackademy

An amazing summer school dedicated to reproducible neuroimaging. You'll find lectures and resources on Python for neuroimaging (Nilearn), data formatting (BIDS), and machine learning applications.
